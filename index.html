Snacking is a sacred ritual. One chip becomes two, and suddenly the bag is half empty and your fingers are dusted in artificial cheese glory. Whether it's a perfectly timed crunch of a pretzel stick or the slow dissolve of a malted milk ball, every bite tells a story — usually one involving procrastination, late-night creativity, or avoiding real responsibilities.

From puffed corn pillows to chocolate-coated regret, snacks are the unofficial currency of focus, boredom, and celebration. You don’t need them, but somehow they're always within arm's reach, whispering sweet nothings like “just one more.”
